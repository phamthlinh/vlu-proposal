Reimagining Career Services & EmployabilityHelping VLUers own their careers from Day 1

01

Context &
Observations

Van Lang University is looking for a holistic approach to support learners to own their careers from Day 1. However, our findings and employer hiring trends show that the entry-level hiring landscape poses unique challenges for candidates in Vietnam.

0%
Employers expect entry-level candidates to have relevant industry experiences.
0%
Employers highly value extra-curricular activities or part-time job experiences.

Although experiences are highly valued, top-tier entry-level hiring in Vietnam is still heavily influenced by university brand names:

The Hard Truth

"A candidate from HUST (Hanoi University of Science and Technology) with a 3.8 GPA was shortcutted directly to the interview phase in the middle of the hiring process and landed the only intern position at VNG, purely based on their university brand."

To improve student employability, typical international models like Columbia and Drexel are often referenced, but they share different contexts when applied to Vietnam:

Reference Model Context & Characteristics Feasibility for VLU & VN Context
TC Columbia Model Ivy League reputation. Large corporations establish exclusive hiring pipelines for Ivy schools, bypassing standard applications. Low Feasibility - Brand dynamics and employer networks are vastly different in Vietnam.
Drexel Co-op Model Manageable co-op scale (~5,000 students/year). U.S. GDP is 62 times higher than Vietnam, promising abundant job opportunities. Low Feasibility - Mandatory long-term internships are difficult to scale within current VLU schedules and local market capacity.
Beyond "Co-op" (CPI Proposed) Every experience counts (clubs, part-time jobs, and short-term project simulations). Helps students build talent pools. Highly Feasible - Fully aligns with VLU's academic strengths, local employers, and Vietnam's entry-level hiring timeline.

Therefore, our recommendation is to think beyond traditional "co-op" models. Every experience counts for employers, meaning clubs and part-time jobs make complete sense; combining them with job simulations and externships will help students understand real jobs and successfully enter companies' talent pools.

02

Project
Objectives

We apply a human-centered design lens to achieve five core objectives for Van Lang University:

  1. Reimagine career services & competency development: Apply a human-centered design lens to shift career offices from passive services to active student launchpads.
  2. Establish expected competency standards: Define a clear picture of employer-expected competencies for entry-level hires, informed by leading national and global frameworks.
  3. Audit VLU student positioning: Review where Van Lang students stand — what they bring, what's missing, and how current services measure up.
  4. Design context-sensitive solutions: Develop a customized model that addresses the employability gap facing Van Lang students in the local market.
  5. Deliver a Minimum Viable Product (MVP): Hand over a functional prototype ready for pilot testing and rapid iteration in the coming semesters.
03

Scope &
Timeline

The project is planned to span **24 weeks**, structured across four sequential phases. Click on each phase below to see specific tasks and activities:

Labor Market Scan & VLU Insights (Weeks 1 - 6)

  • Labor market scan: Map Vietnam employer demand for entry-level hires across key industries, reviewing national frameworks (NVQF) and regional signals (ASEAN, global).
  • Van Lang insights: Map the current student journey, auditing existing career programs, resources, staffing, and reach.
  • Employer survey: Conduct structured interviews and surveys with HCMC employers and HR leads to identify VLU students' employability gaps.
  • University benchmarking: Identify career service models applicable to a private Vietnamese university context from leading universities worldwide.

Co-design & Ideation (Weeks 7 - 12)

  • Co-design workshops: Facilitate collaborative design sessions with students, career advisors, and target employers.
  • Rapid prototyping: Ideate and develop a minimum of 2 separate idea options.
  • Concept evaluation: Assess each concept against impact, relevance, feasibility, and scalability. Shortlist 1 core idea.
  • Leadership sign-off: Present the shortlisted concept to university leadership for final approval and strategic sign-off.

Prototype Development & Testing (Weeks 12 - 18)

  • Build MVP: Create a minimum viable prototype of the selected solution (digital tool, training module, coaching framework, or combination).
  • Develop supporting materials: Create facilitator guides, student-facing materials, and initial platform wireframes.
  • Internal testing & iteration: Test the prototype with a small group of students/advisors; collect feedback and iterate (at least 1 revision cycle).
  • Pilot preparation: Define the cohort size, participating departments, timeline, success metrics, and logistical tools.

Pilot Delivery & Evaluation (Weeks 19 - 24)

  • Pilot delivery: Run the pilot with the defined student cohort, facilitating sessions, activating digital tools, and supporting university staff.
  • Data collection: Gather quantitative data (participation rates, competency self-assessments) and qualitative feedback (advisor notes).
  • Pilot evaluation: Analyze results against defined success metrics to identify what worked, what failed, and prepare recommendations for rollout.
  • Presentation to leadership: Package findings and recommendations for university board review.
04

Pilot
Concepts

Three pilot concepts have been designed to explore different levels of career support. Select a tab to see their details:

Hybrid Model: AI CV Reviewer for Mass + Honors Coaching for Top 5% Elite

This is the highly recommended solution by CPI, combining automated scale with high-impact selective mentoring.

For Mass Students

  • AI CV Reviewer: Online platform giving students instant, actionable feedback on Resumes, Cover Letters, and Portfolios, aligned with local employers' ATS criteria.
  • On-demand Learning: Interactive video courses and resources covering resume writing, LinkedIn optimization, networking, and interview readiness.
  • Case studies: Wonsulting (AI), Coursera (E-learning).

For Elite Students (Top 5%)

  • 1-1 Mentoring: Personalized mock interviews and advanced resume polishing with senior industry recruiters.
  • Honors Program: Interactive workshops on Systems Thinking, workplace AI usage, and solving real corporate case studies.
  • Direct Referrals: Fast-track introduction to top-tier employer partners for premium internship and management trainee roles.

Job Simulation Platform & RMIT-style 1-1 Career Consultations

Focuses on bridging the theory-practice gap with task simulations and professional in-office counseling.

Job Simulation Platform

  • Simulated Work Tasks: Partner with employers to design short, online task simulations (3-5 hours) mirroring daily duties of roles like Marketing Executive or Data Analyst.
  • Talent Pooling: Employers automatically receive a validated pool of students who demonstrate interest and competency.
  • Case study: The Forage.

1-1 Career Consultations

  • Pre-booked Consultations: Schedule 1-1 sessions with professional counselors at the career service office.
  • Career Planning: Individualized guidance for resume roadblocks, salary negotiation, and career path decisions.
  • Case studies: RMIT University, Fulbright University.

Career Management App - One-Stop Employability Hub

A mobile-first portal digitizing all internship opportunities, guides, and networks for VLU students.

Opportunity Hub

  • Integrated Job Board: Curated board for internships and entry-level positions where companies post and students apply directly.
  • Resume & Profile Sync: Syncs with VLU academic records, allowing companies to search candidates based on GPA, department, and skills.

Cẩm nang & Alumni Network

  • Skill Guides: Short blog posts, templates, and video bytes on resume preparation and interview tips.
  • Alumni Directory: Information hub of active VLU alumni, fostering mentor-mentee connections and referrals.
  • Case studies: Handshake, Kinobi.
